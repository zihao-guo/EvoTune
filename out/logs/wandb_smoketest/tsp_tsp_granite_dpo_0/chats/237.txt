This approach considers both edge weights and the overall density of the network to give more accurate prior indicators for edge selection.

In this method, each edge in the input distance matrix is assigned a value based on the ratio of its weight to the average weight of the edges connected to the nodes it connects. The weight is normalized by the sum of weights of all edges connected to these nodes. 

This strategy considers:
1. The actual distance or weight of the edge, which is the direct cost of travel.
2. The connected nodes' edge density, giving a secondary consideration to the robustness of connections.
