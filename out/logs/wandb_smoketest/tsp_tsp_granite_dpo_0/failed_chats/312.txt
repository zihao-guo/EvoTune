```python
        if current_distance > tentative_distances[current_node]:

                heapq.heappush(priority_queue, (tentative_distances[neighbor], neighbor))
    
    # Step 2: Heuristics scoring
    heuristic_matrix = [0] * n
    centrality = [sum(distance_matrix[i]) for i in range(n)]
    max_centrality = max(centrality)
    
    for i in range(n):
        for j in range(i + 1, n):

                heuristic_matrix[i] += 1 / (centrality[i] * centrality[j])
                heuristic_matrix[j] += 1 / (centrality[i] * centrality[j])

    return heuristic_matrix
```

The heuristic score for each node is influenced by both the inverse of the product of the centralities of its connected nodes and its own centrality, which is the sum of distances to all other nodes.

This approach attempts to balance the exploration of more central nodes (those with a large sum of distances to other nodes) and those with a higher proximity to central nodes.
