The triangle inequality principle states that the sum of the lengths of any two sides of a triangle must be greater than or equal to the length of the remaining side. In a graph, this would mean that the direct distance between two vertices should be less than or equal to the distance via a third vertex.

```python
def triangle_inequality_violations(distance_matrix):
    n = len(distance_matrix)
    
    # For each edge (i, j), calculate the difference between the direct distance and the distance via any third vertex
    violations = [[abs(distance_matrix[i][j] - min(distance_matrix[i][k] for k in range(n) if k!= i and k!= j)) for k in range(n) if k!= i and k!= j] for i in range(n)]
    
    # Add these values to the original matrix
    result = [[distance_matrix[i][j] + v for v in vi] for i, vi in enumerate(violations)]
    
    return result
```
